Online Assignment: Wiki Page on Blackboard

Due Date: Sunday, Oct 22, 11:59 pm

Instructions

  • 1 Go to Blackboard page of the course
  • 2 Click on "Course Tools" [left side panel]
  • 3 Click on "Wikis" [look toward the right side of the page]
  • 4 Click on "Midterm Review"
  • 5 Read requirements
  • 6 Click on "Create Wiki Page" on top left corner to post your wiki entry
  • 7 Click on "Edit Wiki Content" on the wiki page not created by you to add content according to requirements below.

Requirements

  • 1 Find one concept discussed in chapters 5, 6, 16, or 17. You can choose one concept from this midterm concept review list here.
  • 2 Create one wiki entry [Click on Create Wiki Page on top left corner] for the concept that you have chosen.
  • 3 In the wiki page that you create, enter the name of the concept in the field "Name" [this is not for your name to be entered. Your name as the author of the wiki post will be displayed automatically by blackboard]
  • 4 Next, write down the definition for the concept in your own words, and find two real-life examples that help you understand and remember the concept clearly, either found in the textbook (if found in textbook, describe the examples in your own words), or on the Internet.
  • 5 Next, If the concept involves discussions such as costs & benefits, controversies, advantages and disadvantages, and/or comparisions to other related concepts, you need to include them in your entry.
  • Next, do either step 6 or 7 only, then, proceed to step 8.
  • 6 Edit one wiki entry that is not created by you to add one more real-life example that helps you understand and remember the concept clearly, either found in the textbook (if found in textbook, describe the examples in your own words), or on the Internet.
  • 7 Another way to edit a wiki post not created by you is to add a discussion regarding costs & benefits, advantages & disadvantages, comparisons with other related concepts, and/or controversies, if any, about the concept in the wiki post.
  • 8 Cite all sources, even the textbook. If there is a source that is mentioned in the textbook that you use (check the references sections of each chapter), you can find that source directly to verify and cite it.

Chapters to Review for Midterm

Chapter 5 Moore's Law; Chapter 6 Disruptive Technologies; Chapter 16 Internet & Telecommunications; & Chapter 17 Information Security
