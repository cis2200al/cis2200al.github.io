Strategy & Technology Cases Exercise

Due date: Friday, September 15, 11:59 pm

Objective: To understand IT-enabled business strategies and their implications for businesses and customers.

What do you need to do?

  • Choose only two (2) questions from the following list, and answer them in a word document. Upload the document in Blackboard.
  • Cite any sources that you use (best to use APA style)
  • Font: Georgia, or Times New Roman
  • Font size: range from 10 pt to 12 pt
  • Spacing: single space
  • Length: for the two questions combined should range from 1 to 2 pages. (it's okay to go over 2 pages, but not okay to be less than 1 full page).

List of questions: (remember, choose only 2 out of these)

  • a Who are the rivals to the Netflix streaming effort? Do any of these firms have advantages that Netflix lacks? What are these advantages?

  • b How does Netflix build its data asset? In what ways does it leverage this data asset? Why is the data asset stronger under Netflix's streaming than it was for Netflix's DVD-by-mail business?

  • c How has Netflix leveveraged crowdsourcing and code contests?

  • d Have you seen a Amazon physical bookstore or kiosk? If so, what was your experience like? (You can visit one near Herald Square area). If not, browse online to find videos or write-ups about these. Based on your experience and/or your online findings, discuss what you think about Amazon's reasons for opening these physical stores/kiosks. Do they make sense? Why, why not, or under what conditions?

  • e Why are grocery stores attractive for Amazon? In what ways is Amazon entering the grocery business and how do these models differ? Are any aspects of Amazon's various grocery models appealing to you? Have you ever shopped for groceries online? If yes, share your experience. If not, detail the reasons why.

  • f Do you use personal cloud services? Do you use Amazon products, or products provided by rivals? Why have you made these choices regarding cloud platforms? Are network effects at work in cloud platforms? How so? What kinds of complementary products might make AWS seem more attractive than a new cloud computing effort?
