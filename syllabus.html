Course Description

  • Class meeting time: Monday 10:45 AM - 12 PM
  • Class location: Room 4-125 NVC

The course introduces students to Information Systems, in business and individual use. The rapid changes in IT and their wide-ranging impacts have made information systems an essential subject that every student, especially business major ones, needs to explore and master.

Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of the course, students will be able to:

  • (1) Explain the basics of Information systems and how Information systems are used in business.
  • (2) Articulate the role that business information systems play in different organizations.
  • (3) Demonstrate literacy in information, technology, and business.
  • (4) Identify business problems and opportunities that can benefit from information systems support.
  • (5) Discuss the crucial role that information systems play in your career as well as in business and society in the 21st century.

BBA Learning Goals

  • (1) Analytical and Technological skills
  • (2) Oral and written communication skills
  • (3) Ethical decision-making
  • (4) Business knowledge integration

Hybrid Model

Please note that this course is offered as a hybrid course. That means, instead of meeting face-to-face for two and half hours every week (both Mondays & Wednesdays), we will be meeting for about an hour and a quarter, only on Mondays. This also means that you will be completing online work to compensate for the hour and quarter for which we will not be meeting face-to-face. The course website will identify the various types of assignments that you should complete outside of the class.

A hybrid class also means that a lot of communication would happen when we are outside of our class. You should visit the course website at least twice a week to check for any updates that I might have posted.

Please note that a hybrid model requires good discipline and time management as there will be work to be done every week. Much of the online work will be fun explorations of technology and will help you learn in a self-paced way.

Assignments, Projects, & Quizzes

Throughout the semester students will be expected to complete homework assignments, which will help them practice the concepts learned in class. The students will also be expected to complete group projects, where they will have to use their skills to solve a complex business problem that requires the development of a spreadsheet and a database.

There will also be ad-hoc in-class questions, exercises, and quizzes. In-class assignments and quizzes have to be submitted before the end of class to receive credit, unless specified otherwise by the instructor.

Instructions for the homework assignments and quizzes will be made available through the course website as and when necessary. Students should periodically check (at least twice a week) the course website for any updates.

Exams

Exams will consist of a combination of true/false and multiple choice questions. Exams will cover material from all aspects of the class sessions (lectures, software demonstrations, discussions, videos, extra handouts, etc.)

Course Policies

Exams

Missing Exams: No make-ups will be given for missed exams. In case of extraordinary circumstances, students who cannot attend an exam must contact the instructor in advance and provide a justification for their absence.

Checking Exam Scores: Students will have an opportunity to check their graded exams but the instructor retains all midterm and final exams.

Final Exam's Time: All students must take the final exam in the time slot posted in the college bulletin.

Exams' content: The exam will include material from both the readings and from the topics covered in the lectures. Some of the lecture material may not be found in the book. Therefore, it is very important to attend classes regularly and keep up with the pace of the reading assignments.

Exam-Taking Conduct: Behavior during exams is expected to conform to Baruch College guidelines. Any form of cheating or communications with other students or any other improper behavior will be dealt according to the guidelines established by the College.

Penalties for cheating range from an F on the exam, to an F on the course, as well as notification to the Dean of the College.

Note:

I take academic integrity very seriously. There will be no form of leniency from me for any kind of cheating or collusion in the violations of exam rules. You are required to check the Academic Integrity statement section below and visit the listed link to read in details the College's policy regarding this matter.

Class Attendance

Class participation is an important element of the course grade, so make every effort to attend class lectures, during which you can participate in answering questions, completing exercises and quizzes.

Classroom Conduct: To avoid disruption, you should arrive at the classroom on time. Cell phones and all other electronic devices should be turned off or at least silenced during class and exams.

In addition, you are expected to be respectful of the instructor and other students during class lectures. Conversations and work unrelated to the class lectures' content need to be reserved for time outside of class.

Missing class: If you miss class, it is your responsibility to find out about any announcements or assignments you may have missed.

Work Submission Standards

Late Work: If you are instructed to submit assignments through blackboard’s assignment submission system, then you must do so before the deadline specified. Late submissions will incur penalties as described in earlier sections or in assignment instructions.

Essential Requirements: Hand-written work will not be acceptable and will earn no credit. As with any other academic submission, students must do their work carefully, striving to achieve high quality. This includes writing clearly, checking spelling and grammar, proofreading the submissions, and handing in the work on or before the specified due date.

Extensions: Extensions may be granted for situations involving illness or personal emergencies. If you need an extension, you must request one in writing or via e-mail before the due date of an assignment.

NOTE: Extensions will not be granted for problems relating to use of home or workplace computer systems (please use the BCTC facilities to avoid such problems).

Work Integrity: Some assignments may require individual work while others may require team work. Any instance of copying, cheating or plagiarism in individual assignments or group projects will result in penalties. Students (or teams) handing in similar work may receive a zero in the assignment/project and/or a failing grade in the course, and will face disciplinary actions.

Research Requirement

You will need to earn 1 hour of out-of-class research-based assignments. The purpose of these assignments is to provide the student with experience in Information Systems research.

Options: There are two options for completing these assignments and you can complete the required credits through a combination of these two options: (a) Participating in Information Systems research studies for 1 hour of participation time; & (b) Writing a 500-word essay on how Information Technology has changed the way in which individuals, businesses or society operates nowadays.

Meaning, you have 3 ways of satisfying the research requirement: (1) You can participate in 1 hour of research studies (there are studies that range from 30 minutes to 1 hour); or (2) write the 500-word essay; or (3) partipate in 30 minutes of research study and write a 250-word essay.

Details about the options are below.

Participation in IS researh studies

You can sign up for IS research studies which range from 30 minutes to 1 hour on the CIS SONA website (http://baruch-cis.sona-systems.com/). Each half an hour segment counts as one point. Therefore, to fulfill the one hour requirement, you must obtain two points in the CIS SONA system.

Please note that the CIS SONA system operates independently from other SONA systems used for other departments (such as Management or Psychology).

You must be at least 18 years of age to participate in a study.

When signing up for studies, please remember: (a) to print the page with the name, date, place, and time of the study and the experimenter’s name (keep this information until the end of the semester); and (b) to cancel though the website before the scheduled time if you cannot make the study.

Log-in Information Your password for the CIS SONA system will be emailed to your Baruch email account within 2 weeks of the start of class. If you forget your password, go to http://baruch-cis.sona-systems.com/ and use the password recovery link on the left hand side of the welcome screen.

Disputes & Participation Decision If you have any disputes about your participation in a study, you should contact the experimenter directly. According to the ethical guidelines, participation is voluntary and individuals may withdraw from a study, at any time, without penalty to that individual.

Writing an essay

References Your essay should include at least one reference from the press or from business magazines. You are required to make sure that your sources are properly referenced in your essay according to the APA guidelines.

Word Count Requirement Remember, 500 words count as 1 hour, and so 250 words count as half an hour.

Submission Essays must be submitted through turnitin.com to be considered as an alternative to the research requirements. Guidelines and submission details are available at the SONA-FAQ section.

Please note that credits earned for other courses in Psychology, Marketing or Management cannot be applied to the IS & Stats Department requirement.

If you have any questions about the CIS Research Requirement guidelines, please send an email to the Participant Pool Manager, Prof. Isak Taksa at: cisresearch@baruch.cuny.edu, or isak.taksa@baruch.cuny.edu.
